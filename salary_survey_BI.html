Salary Survey(POWER BI)

Problem Statement :

The Salary Survey project aims to provide insights into how factors like experience, education, gender, and race influence employee compensation and job satisfaction across various industries and regions. By analyzing this data, organizations can benchmark their compensation strategies, identify diversity gaps, and understand the relationship between experience and salary growth. The goal is to help companies adopt equitable pay practices and foster a more inclusive workplace.

Technologies Used

  • Python    (for data cleaning removing outliers and missing values)
  • Power BI    (for creating the dashboard)

Survey Analysis

Age Distribution

  • Dominant Age Group: The largest group of participants falls within the 30-40 age range, followed by the 20-30 age group.
  • Decline in Older Groups: There's a noticeable decrease in survey representation among individuals aged 50 and above.

Average Annual Salary by Age

  • Highest Earners: Participants between 40-50 years old earn the highest average annual salary, closely followed by the 30-40 and 50-60 age groups.
  • Younger Group Earnings: The 20-30 age group earns the lowest average salary, suggesting that experience and age may contribute to higher earning potential.

Racial Representation by Industry

  • Tech Sector Imbalance: White participants dominate the "Computing or Tech" industry, with Asian individuals having the second-largest representation. Other racial groups have minimal presence in this field.
  • Diversity Challenges: Education and Government/Public Administration sectors show some diversity, with Black and Hispanic individuals being more represented compared to the Tech sector.

Gender Representation by Industry

  • Male-Dominated Fields: Men significantly outnumber women in the "Computing or Tech" industry, indicating gender imbalance.
  • Education Sector Balance: There's a more balanced gender distribution in the Education sector, with women holding a larger share of roles.
  • Inclusivity Gaps: Non-binary and other gender categories are minimally represented across industries, pointing to challenges in inclusivity.

Job Titles and Geographic Representation

  • Broad Job Range: A wide variety of job titles are represented (11.67k), showcasing diversity in roles within the survey.
  • Geographic Limitation: Only 13 countries are represented, indicating a lack of wide geographic diversity in the dataset.

Races and Currencies

  • Diverse Backgrounds: There are 7 distinct racial categories and 9 currencies, reflecting that salary data is drawn from multiple regions and racial backgrounds.

Average Annual Salary by Job Title

  • Voice Actors Lead in Earnings: Voice Actors dominate the salary spectrum, earning close to 20 million—far surpassing other roles.
  • Top Earners: Other high-paying roles include Backend Systems and Overseas Marketing, but their earnings are significantly less than Voice Actors.
  • Lower Average for Marketing and Translation: While jobs in Marketing, Localization, and Japanese Translation are present, they earn considerably less on average.

Count of Race by Country

  • U.S. Dominance: Over 70% of survey participants are from the U.S., heavily influencing the overall data.
  • Global Representation: While countries like Canada, the UK, and Australia contribute meaningfully, others like France, Germany, and New Zealand represent a smaller portion of the dataset.
  • Skewed Insights: This indicates that the salary data may lean toward U.S.-centric results, with limited insights from other regions.

Education Levels

  • Highly Educated Participants: A significant 48.7% of respondents hold a Master’s degree, while 31.8% have a Bachelor's degree.
  • PhDs in the Minority: Those with PhDs or professional qualifications make up a smaller percentage, indicating that the survey skews towards highly educated professionals.
  • Specialized Jobs: This suggests that the roles surveyed tend to be specialized and technical, correlating higher education with more advanced job positions.

Average Salary by Industry

  • Video Games as Top-Paying Industry: The Video Games industry boasts the highest salaries, followed by Customer Service and Commercial Business, reflecting the lucrative nature of these fields.
  • Diverse High-Paying Sectors: Corporate Training, Environment, and Energy also offer high average salaries.
  • Lower-Paying Fields: Conversely, industries like Geology and Multilateral Organizations show lower salary averages.

Count of Jobs by Industry

  • Tech is King: The Computing or Tech industry leads in job representation, making up 16.68% of the total—consistent with its reputation as a dominant sector.
  • Other Key Sectors: Education, Nonprofits, and Government/Public Administration also have a significant presence, though they don’t rank among the highest-paying sectors.
  • Broader Industry Insights: While a wide variety of industries are represented, fields like Healthcare, Engineering, and Finance have fewer roles compared to Tech.

Additional Insights

  • Geographic Representation: The data leans heavily towards U.S. and English-speaking countries, with limited global diversity, especially from non-Western regions.
  • Education and Salary Correlation: The large number of individuals with advanced degrees likely drives the higher salary averages seen in specialized industries.
  • Job Market Focus: Although tech dominates, there is potential for high earnings in sectors like entertainment (Voice Acting, Video Games) and services (Customer Service, Localization).

Average Annual Salary by Total Experience

  • Career Peaks in Mid to Late Stages: Salaries tend to rise as individuals gain more experience, peaking at around 30-40 years of experience. However, there’s a slight dip in average salary for those with over 40 years of experience.
  • Earning Potential: This suggests that the highest earning potential occurs during the mid-to-late career stages, but salaries might taper off in the later years.

Average Monthly Salary by Field Experience

  • Field Expertise Drives Earnings: There’s a clear correlation between field experience and monthly salary. Those with more experience in their specific fields tend to earn higher monthly wages.
  • Outliers with High Earnings: Interestingly, some outliers with slightly less experience still show particularly high salaries, highlighting that expertise and value can sometimes outweigh years of experience.

Average Annual Salary by Field Experience

  • Rapid Growth Early in Career: The data shows that annual salaries rise sharply with 10-20 years of field experience, but begin to plateau or even dip slightly after about 30 years of experience.
  • Marginal Gains for Senior Roles: Early-career professionals experience the most significant salary growth as they gain field expertise, but salary increases become more marginal once seniority is reached.

Count of Race by Gender

  • Balanced Gender Distribution: The gender breakdown among survey participants is relatively even, with men, women, and non-binary individuals each making up around 27% of the population.
  • Room for Racial Diversity: While this gender balance may reflect inclusivity, there’s still an opportunity for greater racial diversity in the survey’s participant pool.

Count of Country by Gender and Work Country

  • Global Representation: The survey shows equal representation across various countries, each contributing around 2.13% of the total data. While this suggests international diversity, it also implies that the data may have been evenly distributed rather than reflecting the actual proportions of the global workforce.
  • Balanced Gender Spread: Gender representation is consistent across different countries, with men and women making up the majority of the workforce, followed by non-binary individuals and others.

Key Insights

For Businesses:

  • Key Trends in Salary Growth:
    • Mid-career professionals (30-40 years of experience) provide the highest return on investment due to peak earning potential.
  • Importance of Field-Specific Expertise:
    • Hiring and nurturing employees with deep domain knowledge significantly drives salary growth.
  • Diversity and Inclusion:
    • There's a need to enhance diversity efforts, particularly in tech-related industries, to ensure balanced racial and gender representation.

For Individuals:

  • Career Growth Dynamics:
    • The strongest career growth occurs in the first 10-20 years, especially for those gaining experience in their specific field.
  • Salary Growth Trends:
    • Salary increases may slow down after reaching a certain level of seniority, indicating the need to focus on expertise building early in one’s career.
  • Educational Impact:
    • Advanced education (Master’s or higher) plays a crucial role in attaining higher-paid roles, emphasizing the importance of continued education and skill development.
